What is Ignite?

Bentonville Public Schools developed the Ignite Professional Studies program to provide students with real, relevant learning experiences in collaboration with regional businesses, non-profits, and industry professionals. The time is now to address a critical component in education - providing students post-secondary options aligned to their passions and strengths.

Ignite is out to change the conversation about preparing students for the opportunities in our community and beyond. Today’s workforce, whether in a professional skilled trade, startup, or Fortune 500 company requires STEM and professional, business, and finance skills. Ignite career strands are designed to align with future opportunities in the community.

Students in Ignite strands build meaningful relationships with mentors in business, industry, and higher education, not only to learn a profession, but also to gain valuable professional skills and entrepreneurial habits of mind necessary for careers in an innovation-based economy.

Computer + Information Technology

Do you love working with computers? Love solving problems and creating solutions using technology? Are you curious about computer coding and the science, technology, engineering, and art behind creating and presenting computer programs, websites or mobile apps to customers? Then the Computer Science and Information Technology strand of the Ignite program is for you!

Bentonville Public Schools will offer a course within the Ignite Progressional Studies Program that immerse students in real, relevant work in Computer Coding and Design. This innovative program meets at the TCS building in Bentonville. Students in the program will gain valuable hands-on experience in the skills and habits of mind necessary for careers and continued education in computer coding, mobile apps development, software development, and website administration. This course is facilitated by a professional IT director and internships are offered at local companies. Students in the program earn certifications from U of A and college credit from NWACC.

Computer Technology is a high-demand, high-wage, high-skilled career in Northwest Arkansas. Employers are looking for students prepared in information technology who have the professional skills and entrepreneurial mindset developed in Ignite Professional Study programs.

John Mark Russell

John Mark Russell is the instructor for Ignite IT Solutions. He holds an undergraduate degree in Computer Science from the University of Southern Mississippi and an M.B.A. from Houston Baptist University. An IT professional with more than 25 years of experience, John Mark started his career as a programmer within Texaco’s Information Systems Department. Upon successful completion of the Systems Engineering program at Electronic Data Systems, he was selected to join the program as an assistant instructor. While working at Global Marine, he managed the software quality assurance program, and worked as a Sybase database administrator. During a 10-year career with Sprint wireless, John Mark lead the IT program management office before moving into a product development role, and eventually accepting a solutions engineer role within Sprint’s server engineering department. A project management opportunity brought John Mark to Walmart in Northwest Arkansas where he was named associate of the month three times while supporting Walmart’s largest software development project.

John Mark initially discovered his passion for teaching while working as a part-time substitute within Bentonville schools. The Ignite IT Solutions instructor role allows him to leverage his professional experience to create an authentic learning environment for students interested in pursuing a career in Information Technology.

Mr. Russell authored the technical report, “The Positive Effects of Robots in American Industry", a technical report in the textbook, Successful Writing at Work, copyright by D.C. Heath and Company.

Scott Fusselman

Scott Fusselman is launching the Creative Arts Media Production program for Ignite. Scott has extensive experience in building new media programs. He developed both the Television Production and Audio/Video Tech and Film Programs at Bentonville High School.

Scott worked professionally for Smith Productions in Tulsa Oklahoma where he helped produce an Off-Road, Four-wheel drive based television program that aired for 7 seasons on the Outdoor Channel.

Scott continues to be involved in freelance videography; working on projects such as Diesel Power Challenge, Peterson’s 4 Wheel, and Off-Road’s Ultimate Adventure. Over the past 5 years, Scott has established a network of professionals who will partner with CA Ignite students to provide real learning experiences. Bentonville High School has produced a distinguished and impressive resume of graduates who are continuing their film/journalism studies at universities such as NYU Film School, Columbia University, University of Arkansas, University of Texas, and John Brown University.

Sarah Bartmier

Sarah Bartmier is a native of Northwest Arkansas where she was raised on a farm near Gravette, AR. Sarah was homeschooled K-12 where her parents encouraged her to explore a variety of personal interests. She specifically loved photography from a very young age. Sarah’s parents held a very high standard for education and becoming a life-long learner.

Sarah began her post-secondary education at Northwest Arkansas Community College. After one year at NWACC, she transferred to Arkansas Tech University in Russellville, AR to pursue a degree in Art Education. Throughout college, Sarah continued to refine her film photography skills through classes and personal exploration.

Sarah began her teaching career as a middle school Art teacher at Spring Hill Middle School. After three years, Sarah accepted a part-time adjunct instructor position at the University of Arkansas in the Apparel Merchandising and Product Development department. This part time position allowed her to pursue a professional photography career. Finally in 2011, Sarah accepted an art instructor position at Bentonville High School where she has taught a variety of subjects including Ceramics 1 and 2, and Success 1.0; a career prep class. Sarah also piloted a high school online Art Appreciation class for Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art. As a graphic designer, Sarah has designed logos, t-shirt, letterheads, and page layouts. Sarah is an Adobe Certified Associate in Visual Communication Using Adobe Photoshop.

The Digital Design and Photography strand is the perfect combination of the three disciplines Sarah is most passionate about; teaching, graphic design, and digital photography. After 12 years as a professional photographer and freelance graphic designer, Sarah is thrilled to begin sharing her experiences and skills with students who share her passion for a career in Graphic Design and Photography. The Ignite program has truly made Sarah’s dream job a reality!

Wendy Broughton

As a former graduate of Bentonville High School, Wendy appreciates the progressive and top notch school district that we live in. She has held several positions in the district. Wendy earned her Bachelors of Nursing degree and Nurse Practitioner license from UAMS in Little Rock. She then went on to obtain her Masters in Nursing Education from the University of Oklahoma.

Wendy started her nursing career at Arkansas Children’s Hospital before moving several times with her husband’s job. She has worked as a floor nurse on a medical surgical unit in a hospital, as a consultant for potential medical malpractice suits with a Chicago law firm, and as a diabetic nurse educator. Wendy also worked in a busy neurology clinic and in a pediatric endocrine clinic. Chris Weeks

Chris Weeks is a third generation carpenter who started working in construction at the age of 12.

Chris’ first teaching experience was as a Construction Technology instructor at a vocational school in Lebanon Missouri. He left teaching to accept a position as a Youth Pastor in Nebraska, but eventually returned to teaching while there.

A licensed contractor for more than 25 years, Chris has worked in many states doing both commercial and residential building. He has built custom homes in the Bentonville area for 12 years.

This is Chris’ 16th year in education and he is honored to help shape the future of his students. The Ignite Construction program includes some of the hardest working and brightest students in the district.

Chris’ love for kids and his experience in building motivates him to teach in the construction field. His goal is to use his God given talents to teach those around him in order to positively impact our town, our state, and ultimately our world.

Teresa Hudson

Teresa was appointed Ignite Program Director in the Fall of 2015. Within one year, she has grown the program to include five career strands; IT Services, Construction Professions, Digital Design and Photography, Digital Media and Medical and Health Sciences. She works closely with Northwest Arkansas Community College to provide concurrent college credit for students enrolled in the Ignite program. Teresa has cultivated partnerships with local businesses that provide real, relevant, professional opportunities for program participants including student internship opportunities.

Prior to Ignite, Teresa served as the District Career and Technical Coordinator for Bentonville Schools where she supervised 11 career-technical specializations. During her tenure she wrote and managed federally and state funded grants for the Career and Technical Programs.

As Virtual Learning Academy Coordinator with Union Public Schools in Tulsa, Teresa developed innovative educational programs including a virtual blended program for Union High School and Intermediate High that involved 850 students taking over 1,200 courses within two years. This program was critically acclaimed and used as a model by other school districts across the country.

Teresa spent over 25 years in Oklahoma teaching business, marketing, and information technology courses. She was highly involved in the career and technical education system serving in professional organizations, National ACTE, OkACTE, ArACTE, and International Society of Technology Educators and sponsoring student organizations Future Business Leaders of America, Business Professionals of America, DECA, Red Cross, Highstepper and Pom Booster Clubs where she traveled across the country allowing her student’s to compete at the national level. Her students have been recognized on the national level in competitive events. She has also spoke about virtual education across the state promoting and advising the need for virtual education options for students.
